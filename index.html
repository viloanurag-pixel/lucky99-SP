The Structure of World Football

Understanding how the major leagues and tournaments are structured is the foundation of any football education.

🏴󠁧󠁢󠁥󠁮󠁧󠁿 Premier League

  • Teams: 20 clubs
  • Season: August – May
  • Format: Home & away, 38 games
  • Relegation: Bottom 3 go down
  • UCL spots: Top 4 qualify

🇪🇸 La Liga

  • Teams: 20 clubs
  • Season: August – May
  • Format: Home & away, 38 games
  • Historic clubs: Real Madrid, Barcelona
  • UCL spots: Top 4 qualify

🇩🇪 Bundesliga

  • Teams: 18 clubs
  • Season: August – May
  • Unique rule: 50+1 fan ownership
  • Historic clubs: Bayern Munich, Dortmund
  • UCL spots: Top 4 qualify

🏆 UEFA Champions League

  • Teams: 36 clubs (new format)
  • Phase 1: League phase (8 games)
  • Phase 2: Knockout from Round of 16
  • Final: Single-match neutral venue
  • Trophy: The Big Ears
Key metrics

Stats That Tell the Story

Modern football is driven by data. Here are the metrics every serious fan should understand.

📐

Expected Goals (xG)

A measure of shot quality. xG assigns a probability to each shot — a tap-in from 3 yards might be 0.9 xG, while a long-range effort might be 0.04 xG. It reveals which teams create genuinely good chances.

🎯

Pressing Intensity (PPDA)

Passes Per Defensive Action measures how aggressively a team presses. A low PPDA means a team wins the ball back quickly after losing possession — the hallmark of elite high-press sides.

🔄

Progressive Passes & Carries

Actions that move the ball at least 10 yards toward goal. These metrics identify ball-carriers and playmakers who advance play purposefully, going well beyond basic pass completion rates.

Common questions

Frequently Asked Questions

What is offside in football? +
A player is in an offside position if they are nearer to the opponent's goal line than both the ball and the second-last defender when the ball is played to them. Being offside only results in a foul if the player is involved in active play. The rule is checked from the moment of the pass, not when the player receives the ball.
How does VAR (Video Assistant Referee) work? +
VAR reviews four types of decisions: goals, penalty decisions, direct red cards, and mistaken identity. The on-field referee can check a monitor at the side of the pitch (an 'on-field review') or accept the VAR team's recommendation. VAR only intervenes for 'clear and obvious errors' — it is not meant to override close calls.
What's the difference between the Champions League and the Europa League? +
The UEFA Champions League is for the top clubs from each European league and is the most prestigious club competition in the world. The Europa League is a second-tier competition with a larger field of clubs from each country. There is also a third tier, the UEFA Conference League, introduced in 2021.
How do points and relegation work? +
In most leagues, teams earn 3 points for a win, 1 for a draw, and 0 for a loss. At the end of the season the top teams qualify for European competition, while the bottom 2-3 teams are relegated to the division below and replaced by the top finishers from the lower league.
What is the World Cup and how often does it happen? +
The FIFA World Cup is the international football tournament held every four years where national teams from around the world compete. 211 nations enter qualifying, and 32 teams (expanding to 48 from 2026) reach the final tournament. It is the most watched single sporting event on earth.
